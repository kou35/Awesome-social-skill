## What Is This 🧠

`Digital souls` refers to the human traces that can be recorded, extracted, transferred, and reused: ways of speaking, habits of judgment, professional experience, emotional responses, fragments of memory, relational roles, and even that very specific sense of "what this person would do." It is not about reducing a person to data. It is about trying to preserve human experience as an interface that can still be interacted with, extended, and invoked again.

`Skills distillation` means breaking down abilities that once lived only in a specific person and turning them into reusable AI assets such as a `skill`, `agent`, `persona`, workflow, or memory system. The goal is not to "clone an all-purpose human," but to distill those genuinely valuable units of experience: how to communicate, how to comfort someone, how to write a weekly report, how to mentor a student, how to save an experiment that is close to collapsing late at night.

Around large models and skill distillation, two emotions often coexist:

> "People working on large models are coding traitors. You have already killed frontend jobs, and now you want to kill backend jobs, QA jobs, ops jobs, cybersecurity jobs, chip jobs, and eventually yourselves and all of humanity."

> "People working on large models are coding gods. You have already liberated frontend work, and now you are going to liberate backend work, QA work, ops work, cybersecurity work, chip work, and eventually yourselves and all of humanity."

This repository chooses to acknowledge that tension directly, while also making its stance clear: we are **human-centered**. The purpose of technology is not to make room for the disappearance of human beings, but to serve them. It is not to flatten human uniqueness, but to preserve, amplify, and pass on the experiences, personalities, and connections that would otherwise fade with time.

So what this repository collects is not just project links, but also a set of questions:

- To what extent can a person's experience be distilled?
- In what form can the companionship inside a relationship continue?
- How can the judgment and style of a role be preserved with respect?
- When we build `digital souls`, how do we keep "human beings" at the center?

## How To Contribute 🤝

Contributions of more skill definitions or open-source projects related to "digital immortality" are welcome. Priority is given to:

- `skill` / `agent` projects with clear role definitions and usage scenarios
- Projects that embody personality, memory, or accumulated professional experience
- Content that can be reproduced, has traceable sources, and ideally includes a repository or demo

Suggested submission format:

```md
| `skill-name` | Role | Use Cases | Core Capabilities |
```
